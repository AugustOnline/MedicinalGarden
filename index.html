Project Summaries

Medicinal Gardening

Mint: Mint is a widely grown herb known for its digestive benefits. I researched its use in teas and foods and tested peppermint oil and mint tea on people after meals. The results showed improved digestion and reduced bloating for many.

Ginger: Ginger has strong anti-inflammatory properties and helps soothe digestion and sore throats. I experimented with ginger water taken before meals, which helped reduce stomach discomfort for most participants. Growing ginger indoors was also successful.

Aloe Vera: Aloe vera is commonly used for skin healing, especially burns and irritations. I applied aloe gel from fresh leaves to minor skin redness and saw faster recovery compared to untreated areas. I also learned how to propagate aloe from a leaf cutting.

I designed and conducted all tests, documented results carefully, and maintained all the plants throughout the project.

Plant Care Tips

Mint

Mint grows best in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Water regularly but avoid waterlogging. Prune frequently to encourage bushier growth.

Ginger

Ginger thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect sunlight. Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy. Plant rhizomes horizontally about 2 inches deep.

Aloe Vera

Aloe vera prefers bright, indirect light and dry conditions. Water sparingly, allowing soil to dry completely between waterings. Use well-draining cactus soil for best results.

Medicinal Plant Recipes & Uses

Mint Tea

Steep fresh mint leaves in hot water for 5-7 minutes. Drink after meals to aid digestion and reduce bloating.

Ginger Water

Slice fresh ginger and boil in water for 10 minutes. Drink a small cup before meals to help reduce nausea and inflammation.

Aloe Vera Gel

Cut a thick aloe leaf, scoop out the gel, and apply directly to minor burns or skin irritations to soothe and promote healing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can mint be grown indoors?

A: Yes, mint grows well indoors in pots with indirect sunlight and regular watering.

Q: How long does it take for ginger to grow?

A: Ginger can take 8-10 weeks before you see significant growth above soil.

Q: Can I regrow aloe vera from a leaf?

A: Yes, by letting the leaf dry to form a callus before planting, aloe can regrow roots and a new plant.

Q: Are these plants safe for everyone?

A: Generally yes, but always check for allergies or consult a doctor before medicinal use.
